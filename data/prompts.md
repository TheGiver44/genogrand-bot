Dogs: Coins like Dogecoin, Shiba Inu, and Floki Inu use dog imagery, especially Shiba Inu breeds, as they represent loyalty and cuteness. These images often have cartoonish, exaggerated expressions to enhance their humorous appeal.

Cats and Other Animals: Some coins use cats or other animals with expressive or funny faces, especially if these animals have internet meme recognition (like Grumpy Cat).
2. Cartoonish and Simplified Designs
Bold Lines and Vibrant Colors: Memecoins usually use colorful and straightforward cartoon styles that are easily recognizable and visually engaging.
Character-Based Designs: Some coins create distinct characters or mascots (like Pepe for $PEPE coin or Wojak for $WOJAK coin), which are either a play on existing internet icons or custom-made personalities that relate to the coin’s community and narrative.
3. Retro and Pixel Art
Pixel Art: Popular among coins targeting nostalgia and simplicity, pixel art styles often pay homage to old video games, appealing to tech-savvy audiences.
Minimalist and Retro Vibes: Certain projects lean on retro computer graphics and old-school aesthetics to stand out visually and attract users who are fans of retro gaming or internet culture.
4. Cultural and Viral References
Internet Memes and Trends: References to viral internet culture (such as Pepe the Frog or Wojak characters) make the project more recognizable and shareable. These often involve simple yet exaggerated facial expressions that convey relatable emotions like joy, sadness, or greed.
Trendy, Relatable Phrases or Symbols: Many projects capitalize on popular slang or symbols within crypto culture, like “to the moon” or “diamond hands,” using visual elements that emphasize these sayings (e.g., rockets, diamond hands).
5. Humorous and Satirical Branding
Parodies of Existing Brands or Icons: Many memecoins create parodies of famous brands, characters, or pop culture references to make the project seem lighthearted and approachable.
Satirical Art: Projects like $ASS (Australian Safe Shepherd) or $ELON (Dogelon Mars) use satire, poking fun at industry trends, influencers, or the idea of “moonshot” investing in crypto.
